Research Questions¶

# STABILITY ANALYSIS: Year-to-Year Correlation

# Calculate year-over-year correlation
years = sorted(df['Year'].unique())
correlations = []

for i in range(len(years)-1):
    year1_data = df[df['Year'] == years[i]].set_index('Country')['Score']
    year2_data = df[df['Year'] == years[i+1]].set_index('Country')['Score']
    
    common_countries = year1_data.index.intersection(year2_data.index)
    if len(common_countries) > 0:
        corr = year1_data[common_countries].corr(year2_data[common_countries])
        correlations.append({
            'Year1': years[i],
            'Year2': years[i+1],
            'Correlation': corr
        })

corr_df = pd.DataFrame(correlations)
avg_corr = corr_df['Correlation'].mean()

print("Year-to-Year Correlation (Temporal Stability):")
print(corr_df.round(3))
print()
print(f"Average correlation: {avg_corr:.4f}")
print()
print("Interpretation: Press freedom scores show very high temporal stability.")
print("Country positions tend to change gradually rather than abruptly.")
print(f"This suggests RSF measurements are reliable and consistent over time.")

Executive Summary¶

Key Findings¶

• Global Decline: Global average press freedom deteriorated by approximately 2.7 points between 2013 and 2021.

• Temporal Stability: Press freedom scores exhibit extremely high year-to-year stability (average correlation = 0.991), indicating reliable measurements.

• Regional Differences: Statistically significant regional differences exist (Kruskal–Wallis test, p < 0.001, effect size ε² = 0.478).

• Best Improvers: Somalia, Sudan, and Sri Lanka showed the largest improvements in press freedom scores.

• Deteriorating Rapidly: Tajikistan, Libya, and Poland showed the largest deteriorations.

• Natural Clustering: K-Means clustering identified four distinct country profiles based on average press freedom score, score volatility, and long-term trend direction.

Technical Approach¶

  • Data Source: Reporters Without Borders (RSF) via Our World in Data (179 countries, 2013–2021)
  • Statistical Methods: Kruskal–Wallis test for regional comparisons, K-Means clustering for country grouping
  • Temporal Analysis: Year-over-year correlation assessment and long-term trend calculation
  • Validation: Silhouette score assessment of cluster quality
